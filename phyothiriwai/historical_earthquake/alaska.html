1964 Good Friday , Alaska Earthquake

Earthquake in US

The 1964 Good Friday Earthquake was a magnitude 9.2 megathrust quake in Alaska , the most powerful in US history , killing 131 people , reshaping the landscape , and generating deadly tsunamis that spurred modern tsunami warning systems. Occurred on March 27 , 1964 at 5:36 p.m. The quake lasted approcimately 4 minutes and 38 seconds, rupturing over 600 miles of fault and releasing nearly 500 years' worth of accumulated stress.
